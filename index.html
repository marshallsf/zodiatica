How to Play — Quest to Zodiatica

🎯 The Goal

Be the first player to get rid of all your cards. On your turn you play one card from your hand onto the discard pile by matching it to the card on top. Can't match? You draw. Empty your hand and you win.

👥 Setup

  • 2–6 players — any mix of humans and AI.
  • Each player is dealt 5 cards; one card is flipped face-up to start the discard pile.
  • The rest form the draw pile. When it runs out, the discard is reshuffled automatically.

🃏 How matching works — the heart of the game

Every card shows up to three icons you can match on: its center subject and its two top-corner badges. Each icon is a Sign, Planet, Element, or Mode.

Your card is playable if it shares at least one icon with the top card of the pile. For example, if the pile shows AriesTaurusSaturn, you can play any card that shows Aries, Taurus, or Saturn anywhere on it.

💡 This is exactly how the physical deck plays — match by the corners and center, not by astrological rules. The badges are printed on each card.

🎟️ Reading the chips

To make matches obvious, each card shows little chips for its icons. The chips under the discard pile are what you must match. On your hand cards:

  • A chip that glows cyan = it matches the pile (this card is playable).
  • Faded chips = don't match. If all of a card's chips are faded, you can't play it.
  • A gold WILD ✦ chip = a wild card (always playable).

☀ ☾ Special matches

Some cards form their own families that always match each other, even if their badges differ:

  • Sun group — Sun, Solar Eclipse, Sunrise, and Sunset all match one another.
  • Moon group — Moon, Full Moon, Void Moon, Moon Signs, and Lunar Eclipse all match.
  • Rising Sign — a connector card; play it off either of its two badges.

✦ Wild cards (Elements & Modes)

The 5 Elements (Fire, Water, Air, Earth, Spirit) and 3 Modes (Cardinal, Fixed, Mutable) are wild: you can play one on any card, even when nothing else matches.

  • When you play a wild, it sets the new thing to match — its element or mode.
  • Bonus: right after a wild, you may immediately play one more card from your hand (or skip). Great for dumping a stubborn card.

↩️ Retrograde cards

The 8 planet Retrograde cards play like their planet, but also reverse the turn order — a little Mercury-retrograde chaos. With 2 players that simply bounces play back and forth.

🪐 Planet X

Planet X is a normal card (not wild) — match it by its printed badges. Several other cards carry a “Planet X” badge, so it connects in surprising places.

🔄 When you can't match

Press Draw card to take one from the pile. If the drawn card matches you may play it right away; otherwise press Pass and play moves on.

🏆 Winning

The moment you play your last card, you win and the cosmos celebrates. (Down to one card? Everyone gets a heads-up in the log.)

🎛️ Controls & options

  • Draw / Pass — your turn actions.
  • 🔊 (top-right) — mute or unmute music & sound.
  • Guided mode — highlights playable cards and shows a hint each turn. Great for learning.
  • AI difficulty — Easy (careless), Normal (saves wilds), Hard (saves wilds & plans its hand).
  • Hot-seat — with 2+ humans, a “pass the device” screen hides each hand between turns.

🧠 Tips

  • Hold your wild cards for when you're truly stuck — they're your escape hatch.
  • Play hard-to-match cards early; keep flexible, well-connected cards for later.
  • Use a wild's bonus play to offload a card that shares nothing with the pile.
